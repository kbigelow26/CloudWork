Attraction do's and don'ts

There are types of attractions for everyone, and you do not want to make the mistake of paying for each one individually.

Having spent years watching tourists make the mistake of thinking "I will not do all of the attractions listed on this pass, I should just pay individually"; I have learned many tricks in planning to visit attractions in Niagara Falls. The Passes offered by the Niagara Parks Commission are the best bet to get your fun at a low price. Even if you skip an attraction on the list it is still cheaper as the deal gives a discounted price for all. If you come during the off season you can get these passes for an even better price. In the off season the locals are trying to get any money that they can, so discounts are everywhere!

Do your research, figure out which attractions are a must see for you. Then look at the passes that are offered through the Niagara Parks Commission, and save yourself the money

To save your long wait times you can talk to a person at the Welcome Centre located at Table Rock and book your times. That way you can enjoy the view of the falls for longer without having to wait in long lines in the heat.

All attractions let you bring a small bag on them so remember to bring a water bottle! You do not want to pay the impossible price of water, or even wait in the long line to get it.

The most important thing to remember when doing the attractions at Niagara Falls it to have fun and enjoy your time away from home.
